⚠️ Open questions about this model
Reviewed as a relational database (so every relationship must have a foreign key), and framed as "Don't Make Me Think" violations — each is a spot where the reader has to stop and guess. ⚠️ markers on the cards point to the exact field.
1 · Get rid of question marks — every pointer should land somewhere obvious
  • override_actor_id points to a ReviewActor that doesn't exist as an entity.
  • producer_id, underwriter_id, actor_id, initiated_by — no Person/Actor entity for any of them to point to.
  • paid_to_party_id — is the payee the customer, or a repair shop / third party? No entity says.
2 · Don't make me guess the type
  • resolves_to_node_id lands on 3 different node types — how is the target type known?
  • causal_trigger_event_id is "PolicyTransaction or ClaimEvent" — which one?
  • :RECORDS and :CAUSAL_LINK say "any node" — no constraint at all.
3 · A name should mean one thing
  • coverage_id is the PK on both PolicyCoverage and ClaimCoverage.
  • item_id is the PK on both InsuredItem and ClaimItem.
4 · Don't make me hunt for the connection
  • 13 of the 27 entities have NO relationship at all in the document — they stand completely alone. Flagged ⚠️ on the card header.
  • The document never says how a ClaimEvent reaches its items / coverages / payments — those relationships simply aren't written down.
5 · Show me the thing I actually came for
  • Loss ratio can't be computed — no exposure (vehicle-months), no earned_premium, no incurred total.
6 · Relationships with no linking key (relational view)
  • 6 of the 14 declared relationships have no foreign key — so the two tables can't actually be joined. They're marked NO KEY in the table below.
  • Example: PolicyDocument and DocumentClassification share no ID — nothing says which label belongs to which document.
  • Worst one: a ClaimEvent has no policy_id, so a claim can't be tied to the policy it was filed under.
  • Inconsistent: some relationships DO carry a key (resolves_to_node_id, rule_id, retention_schedule_id) while these 6 carry nothing.
  • Source: the relationships are the document's own (Section 4.2 "Core Edge Taxonomy") — but the entity tables don't carry the keys to implement them.

⚠️ Problems
Reading the relationships: each row is From · relationship · To. DOC = one of the 14 relationships formally defined in the document (Section 4.2). Every relationship shown here comes straight from the document — nothing is inferred or added. NO KEY = the relationship is declared but no foreign key joins the two tables — broken in a relational database. Two edges are polymorphic (connect to any node): :RECORDS (audit trail) and :CAUSAL_LINK (typed cause→effect). PK = primary key · FK = points to another entity.
